In 1990, The company's board approves a new strategy for IBM. The corporation that was famous for business machines set out to become "a world-class services company." This was primarily done to shift away from competitors in the Personal Computer industry such as Compaq and Dell, who were making significant gains using Microsoft's operating system. IBM's shift to a service based business would differ from their previous model greatly.
IBMÂ’s traditional sales force was product-focused & organized by geography. Since services customers increasingly demanded IT vendors to be industry savvy, IBM trained its sales force accordingly. This led to a reorganization of sales by industry, creation of a new role for services brand specialists to feed opportunities to the services group, & development of a consultative sales approach. The company also redeveloped its channel partner program to target small & mid-size businesses with sales & distribution processes. In 2005, IBM sold its hardware buiness to Lenovo who still uses many IBM brand names for product recognition.
IBM has been around almost decade & like any company with such longevity, IBM has had to adapt to a constant, changing markets. IBM has reduced its dependence on hardware & has repositioned itself in software & supply chain services as a world leader. IBM has reinvented itself as a global company, who expands into 160 countries. IBM was able to effectively shift market strategies in order to remain relevant and prosperious in a quickly shifting industry.
