History of IBM

IBM has come a long way from its humble beginings. It was founded in 1911, under the name of Computer Tabulating Recording Company. In 1925, They would change their name to International Business Machines. Its early accomplishments included helping in World War II by keeping statistcs. These early machines used punch cards to record informaton. In 1944, They would develop their first computer known as the Automated Sequence Controlled Calculate, also known as the Mark I. This machine was used by the Navy to calculate gun trajectories.
In the 1950's, IBM began producing computers primarily for the government and business. THese computers were extremely large and used for basic operations.They were not yet available for the general public. In 1980, IBM made a deal with MicroSoft that would change the computer industry forever.Microsoft would put their operation system on IBM computers, changing the way computers were used. A year later in 1981, IBM would release their first ever personal computer called Personal Computer 5150. Eventually, many personal computer clones would be released in the decade, and Microsoft would provide operating systems for them. This would lead to a decline in IBM's monopoly on the personal computer industry.
IBM also pioneered many other technologies. in 1996, scientits at IBM created Deep Blue, a chess playing computer that would eventually win the world championship in chess. The architecture in Deep Blue would go on to help the computer industry in other fields such as data analysis and artificial intelligence. It also showed the potential of human and artificial intelligence interaction. Ultimately, Deep Blue was retired by IBM. It inspired other projects at IBM such as the Watson project, a robot that played Jeopardy. The implications of the Deep Blue project can still be seen today, as more complex and powerful computers are emerging.
