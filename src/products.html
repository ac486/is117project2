Products & Services

IBM's original products in the computing world were large mainframe computers used by governments and corporations to perform calculations. In 1980, They would release the Personal Computer 5150 which was their first computer made available to the public. It was sold at a retail price of $1565. IBM modeled its marketing strategy and pricing after Apple's personal computer.The Personal Computer 5150 was large, featured a floppy disk drive and a low amount of RAM. However, the PC as it was known, was extremely successful and would set the standard for Personal Computers.
Another innovative product produced by IBM was the ThinkPad, one of the earliest laptop computers. The ThinkPad was developed in 1991. It was built to be reliant and resilient. The ThinkPad also featured a Trackpoint pointing device in the center of the keyboard as a substitution for the mouse. The ThinkPad would become a success and receive many awards for its design and quality. It was one of IBM's last big hardware successes before its shift to a service based company.
Today most of IBM's products are services provided to businesses. This includes consulting service and analytical software, offering wide variety of technology, products, & services to business customers throughout the world.
